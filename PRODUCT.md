# GoLift

## Register

product

## Users

Portuguese-speaking individual athletes and fitness enthusiasts who train regularly (at the gym, at home, or outdoors). They want to track workouts, monitor progress over time, and get AI-powered training plans tailored to their goals. The user is self-motivated but values structure, data visibility, and occasional encouragement. Mobile-first — the app is used in and around training sessions, not at a desk.

## Product Purpose

GoLift is a personal fitness companion that helps users log workouts, track metrics (strength, volume, streak, body stats), and follow AI-generated training plans. It exists because most fitness apps are either too simple (just a timer) or too complex (full coach-management platforms). GoLift sits in the middle: powerful enough for serious trainees, simple enough to open and log a set in 10 seconds. Success means users open it daily, check their streak, log consistently, and see real progress over weeks and months.

## Brand Personality

**Sleek · Modern · Premium**

Voice is direct and motivating — never pushy, never gimmicky. The tone is confident but not bro-y ("you've got this", not "NO PAIN NO GAIN"). Portuguese language throughout, with a European Portuguese feel. The app should feel like a premium tool you're proud to open mid-workout: fast, polished, quietly authoritative. Inspired by Apple Fitness+ in visual refinement and motion quality, but distinct in being more data-forward and training-focused.

## Anti-references

- **Dark/gritty "fitness bro" aesthetic** (shredded silhouettes, neon accents, aggressive typography). GoLift is for all trainees, not only bodybuilders.
- **Cluttered, ad-heavy interfaces** with too many upsells. The app should feel spacious and focused.
- **Over-gamified toys** (pointless animations, cartoon achievements). Gamification (streaks, XP, levels) should feel earned and meaningful, not infantilising.
- **SaaS-cream / generic warm-neutral backgrounds** — the Apple Fitness+ inspiration means a darker, richer, more dramatic feel. Light mode exists but dark mode is where the app shines.

## Design Principles

1. **Motion with purpose.** Every animation serves function: showing state change, guiding attention, or giving feedback. No decorative bounce or gratuitous transitions. Reduced-motion users get the same information, just without the animation.

2. **Data as fuel, not noise.** Metrics, charts, and progress indicators are the core value. Surface the right number at the right time — always the headline figure, the trend, and the next step — without overwhelming. One clear number beats a dashboard of noise.

3. **Premium restraint.** Fewer UI elements, more considered. Every card, button, and label earns its place. White space is a feature. The app feels expensive because it doesn't try to fill every pixel.

4. **Mobile-native, not web-in-a-box.** The primary interaction is thumbs-on-glass during or between sets. Tap targets are generous. Navigation is thumb-reachable. The UI never assumes a keyboard or mouse.

5. **Dark mode is the hero.** The app is used in gyms, early mornings, and evenings — low-light environments. Dark mode is not an afterthought but the primary canvas. Light mode is clean and maintains full contrast, but the visual identity is built for dark.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Body text contrast ≥4.5:1 against its background. Touch targets ≥44px. All motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`. Support for dynamic type / system font scaling on iOS. Colour is never the sole carrier of information (patterns, labels, and icons accompany colour-coded states). Portuguese-language interface throughout.
